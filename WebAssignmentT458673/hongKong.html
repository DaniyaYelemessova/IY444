Victoria Peak (太平山頂) – A Historical Landmark of Hong Kong

Victoria Peak, commonly known as "The Peak," is the highest point on Hong Kong Island at 552 meters (1,811 feet) above sea level. It is one of the most iconic landmarks in Hong Kong, offering breathtaking panoramic views of the city's skyline, Victoria Harbour, and the surrounding islands.



    Historical Significance - Colonial Era (1842–1941)


  • After Hong Kong became a British colony in 1842, Victoria Peak became a prestigious residential area for high-ranking British officials and wealthy expatriates.
  • Due to its cooler climate and fresh air, it was considered an escape from the heat and humidity of the lower city.
  • In the late 19th century, the British government restricted Chinese people from living on The Peak, making it an exclusive area for Europeans.

    Interesting facts


  • The original Peak tramcars were made of wood and powered by coal-fired steam engines.
  • Before the tram was built, residents used sedan chairs carried by porters to travel up and down the Peak.
  • The Peak offers one of the best night views in the world, especially from the Sky Terrace 428.
