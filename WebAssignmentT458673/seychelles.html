Okavango Delta: Botswana

What Makes the Okavango Delta Special?

The Okavango Delta is home to a vast array of wildlife. The area is a paradise for nature lovers and wildlife enthusiasts alike. During your visit, you might encounter:


  • African elephants: The delta is home to some of the largest elephant herds in Africa. These majestic creatures roam freely through the lush landscapes.
  • African wild dogs: The endangered African wild dog can also be found here. These highly social creatures often live in packs and have fascinating hunting strategies.
  • Lions and leopards: The Okavango is known for its big cats, including lions, leopards, and cheetahs. Spotting one of these apex predators is a thrilling experience!
  • Birdwatching: Over 400 bird species make the Okavango Delta their home, making it a birdwatcher's paradise.

Best Time to Visit the Okavango Delta

Season Months Highlights
Dry Season (Peak Flooding) June - October Best for wildlife viewing, mokoro safaris, fewer mosquitoes, warm days, cool nights.
Green Season (Rainy) November - April Lush scenery, great for birdwatching, newborn animals, lower water levels.
Shoulder Season May & Early November Transition period, rising water levels (May), good wildlife before rain starts (Nov).
