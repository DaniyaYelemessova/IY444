Must-Visit Spot in New York

This website is created by Daniya Yelemessova ♥ based on my personal travel experiences.

Location: Liberty Island and Ellis Island

Visiting the Statue of Liberty is an amazing experience, but there are a few things I learned from my trip that can make your visit even better.

  • Prepare for Seasickness – If you get seasick easily, I highly recommend taking motion sickness pills before the ferry ride. The water can be rough, and without them, your experience might be uncomfortable - just like mine was the first time!
  • If you don’t read about its history beforehand or take a guided tour, the visit might feel like just walking around and taking pictures.
  • Wear Comfortable Shoes – You’ll be walking a lot, so comfortable shoes are a must! Plus, the lines for the ferry can be very long, and you might have to stand for a long time before boarding.

A Little Bit of History for You

The Statue of Liberty became a symbol of hope for immigrants arriving in the U.S. in the late 19th and early 20th centuries. As millions passed through Ellis Island, they saw the statue as a welcoming sign of freedom and opportunity in America. It stood as a beacon for those seeking a better life, marking the start of their journey in a new land.

Facts:

  • The statue was designed by Frédéric Auguste Bartholdi and dedicated on October 28, 1886.
  • The Statue of Liberty stands 93 meters tall, from the base to the tip of the torch.
  • It has greeted over 12 million immigrants as they arrived through Ellis Island.
